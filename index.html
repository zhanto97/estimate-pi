Approximating \( \pi \) with Monte Carlo method

Suppose there is a square of side length \(2r\) and a circle of radius \(r\) inscribed in this square. Now, the main idea is to generate a random sample of points on this square and count the number of points that happen to be lying inside the circle.
$$\text{Area of the circle: } \pi r^2 \;\; \text{Area of the square: } 2r*2r = 4r^2$$ $$ \frac{\text{area of circle}}{\text{area of square}} \approx \frac{\text{number of points inside circle}}{\text{total number of points}} $$ for very large sample. It follows that we can approximate \( \pi \) as $$ \pi \approx 4*\frac{\text{number of points inside circle}}{\text{total number of points}} $$ Let's see this in action!
